8-Day Home Workout Plan

My workout plan for training at home with 15 lb dumbbells

Program Overview

Duration

I follow 8 days of progressive home training

Frequency

I train 4-5 days per week

Focus

I focus on bodyweight movement, light dumbbell strength, and safe teen-friendly conditioning

Goal

I build strength and endurance using at-home equipment only

8-Day Cycle

  • Day 1: Upper Body Strength
  • Day 2: Lower Body Power
  • Day 3: Rest or Light Cardio
  • Day 4: Upper Body — Muscle Growth
  • Day 5: Lower Body — Muscle Growth
  • Day 6: Full Body Conditioning
  • Day 7: Active Recovery
  • Day 8: Power & Explosiveness

Day 3: Rest or Light Cardio

Recovery day - I focus on active recovery and mobility work.

Active Recovery Protocol

  • I do 20-30 minutes of light cardio (walking, cycling, swimming)
  • I spend 10-15 minutes on stretching and mobility work
  • I foam roll for 5-10 minutes
  • I can do yoga or pilates if I want
  • I focus on relaxation and recovery

Day 7: Active Recovery

Recovery day - I focus on mobility and light movement.

Recovery Protocol

  • I do 15-20 minutes of light walking or yoga
  • I follow a comprehensive stretching routine (20 minutes)
  • I use foam rolling and myofascial release for 10-15 minutes
  • I do meditation or breathwork for 5-10 minutes
  • I avoid intense training on this day
  • I focus on mental and physical recovery

Reflection

What is the primary focus of today's workout?

Today my primary focus is building upper body strength and control using bodyweight movements and light dumbbells. I am also working on improving my stability, posture, and muscular endurance.

How is my workout helping me progress to achieving the fitness goals I set in class?

This workout helps me progress by developing consistent strength, increasing my endurance, and reinforcing good form with home-friendly exercises. It keeps me on track with the fitness goals from class by using a balanced plan that improves my overall athletic ability and keeps me motivated to train regularly.

Important Tips for Success

Nutrition

I eat in a slight surplus (300-500 calories) with adequate protein (1g per pound of bodyweight)

Sleep

I get 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night for optimal recovery

Hydration

I drink plenty of water throughout the day, especially before, during, and after workouts

Tracking

I keep a workout log to track weights, reps, and progress over time

Consistency

I stick to the program for all 8 days to see optimal results

Equipment

I use bodyweight and light dumbbells only. I keep weight under 15 lb and choose exercises that feel safe and controlled.

Safety

I warm up properly and use good form. I consider working with a coach if I am new to these movements.
